### Policy forms:
1. Named Peril Form (IBC 4036) - basic, limited coverage; blueprint for further modifications. To get paid - damage has to occur from following conditions 
	 - Fire.
	
	-  Lightning, including loss or damage to electrical devices.
	
	- Explosion (limited).
	
	- Impact by aircraft, spacecraft, or land vehicles.
	
	- Riot, vandalism, or malicious acts.
	
	-  Smoke due to a sudden, unusual, and faulty operation of any stationary furnace.
	
	-  Leakage from fire protective equipment.
	
	- Windstorm or hail.
*** 
- Broad Form (IBC 4037) - covers all physical disasters. Exclusions:
	- Earthquake 
	- Flood 
	- Seepage & leakage 
	- Backup/overflow of water from sewers 
	- Entrance of rain, snow through openings 
	- Mechanical or electrical breakdown 
	- Failure to control temperature 
	- Trade loss 
	- Pollution & contamination 

- Clauses, exempted from broad form:
	1. Debris removal after insured condition occurred (not provided if limit is reached)
	2. Reinstatement Clause - amount of insurance money is **fixed** for duration of policy, regardless of claim quantity 
	3. Subrogation - pay insured and then sue someone who caused the troubles 
***

#### Main physical risks:
1. Building - to self-explanatory include: trees inside the building  
2. Stock - merchandise and packaging, similar property belonging to other which insured obligated to take care 
3. Equipment - content usual to insured's business; similar to what insured has, which was promised to be taken care; betterment to the building

### Risk analysis (COPE)
1. Construction - building *built* with modern materials +age of facility 
2. Occupancy - its density and type affect the fire rate; type of content & manufacturing process 
3.  Protection - availability and type of fire protection(water supply, response time), indoor monitoring and prevention systems 
4. Exposure:
	1. Location - what is located to the left, right, etc. 
	2. Claim history - for insured and similar businesses

### Business interruption insurance - restore income to before loss & pays business ongoing expenses  
1. Gross profit - assumes that business returns to initial position **immediately** after re-opening 
2. Income - assumes that business requires some time to get customers/contracts back 
3. Extra expense - **does not insure loss of income**, forms insures necessary extra expenses related to interruption. Covers expenses over and above normal OpEx   
	1. Renting temporary premise & equipment 
	2. Overtime salaries 

	3. Insured purchase single limit and it spreads over defined period 
	4. *This form is added to either Gross or Profit forms*

### Surety Bonds - eliminates risk to someone who ordered a work

#### Parties:
1. Principal - liable party; contractor 
2. Obligee - someone else is obligated too(requester) 
3. The surety - someone who promises to pay if principal fails to deliver 

#### Details:
1. Bond premium is charged - service fee
2. Principal is liable to surety; in case of principal failure - surety fulfills the promise - then seeks reimbursement 
3. Bond limit or penalty for failure
	1. Unchanged throughout the contract 
4. Surety is an indeterminate length and non-cancellable
