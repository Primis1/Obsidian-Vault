### Third party Liability - pays if someone is hurt, killed or there is a property damage. Protects against law suit
- 
	- Covers both claims and court fees(on top of limit) 
	- Min amount is 200k 
-  Absolute liability - protect third party from injuries despite breach of contract
	- Drank driver hits someone - insurer ***still*** response to injured person
- Threshold - all injured claim compensation under their own insurance policy(no-fault system)
	- Right to sue is available after **threshold(of injury severity**)
	- Falls under Tort law
- Pecuniary loss - loss of wages due through driver's negligence 
	- You **can sue** without a *Threshold* for **Pecuniary loss**Â (i.e. loss of income).
- Non-pecuniary loss - physical damages

### Accidental Benefit - benefit if *you* are injured inside the car, regardless of fault. Ensures that there are minimum funds available for medical bill of anyone injured 
- Statutory accident benefit schedule(SABS
- OAP 1 won't pay addition to injured own life insurance

#### Payment priority:
1. Insured person in own vehicle -> own insurer 
2. Uninsured person in insured vehicle(accident) -> insured vehicle policy 
3. Uninsured person & uninsured vehicle -> any other insured vehicle in an accident 
4. Uninsured person without any insured vehicle -> Motor Vehicle Accident Claim Fund

### Direct compensation For Property Damage - covers damages to your automobile, if no-fault of yours(if it is - coverage from collision coverage applies)
- By using DCPD you surrender your rights to sue inflictor
- Compensation is based on fault(50/50, 20/80)
- Applies only within Ontario
- If fault is your - optional **Collision** coverage takes place of DCPD; in 50/50 case money ll be **drawn from both coverage**
- ##### Submit proof of loss within 90 days

### Loss / damage to own vehicle - Optional Coverage; possible to make *All Peril Coverage* 
- Exempt from paying under illegal use
- Each claims has a deductible 
- #### If claim splits DPCD & Collision - deductibles from both are applied for each sum
	- Excluded deductible for fire & lightning 
- Choice of reimbursement is with insurer 
- Content of vehicle is excluded(overlap with DCPD)
- Insurer will pay & won't subrogate if car was lend to friend(unless he engaged in illegal activity)
- **Theft** must be reported after 72h, 900$ for renting a car  

#### Exclusions:
1. Wear & tear 
2. Content of trailer 
3. Theft by the household member or home employee(unless full coverage is purchased)

### Endorsements:
#### OPCF 5 - for lease of vehicle, allows individuals to extend their auto policy to leased vehicle 

#### OPCF 20 - coverage for replacement car 

#### OPCF 23 A & B - debtor protection 
#### OPCF 27 - coverage for non-owned vehicle 

#### OPCF 32 - recreational vehicle by unlicensed operator. Under Statutory Condition 4(1) - driving such vehicle is prohibited - however endorsement covers it +anyone under age 16 

#### OPCF 38 - electronic accessories, increase limit to 1500$

#### OPCF 43 - removing depreciation from claim; insurer pays lesser of a) purchase price b) manufacturer list price at time of purchase 

#### OPCF 44R - family extension for bodily injury coverage. Pays difference between own liability coverage(1m), claim & underinsured coverage(200k) = 800k of compensation for physical damage or death of a family member  

#### OPCF 49 - opt out of DCPD; premium economy
