### Article 2 General Provision 

1. Location - should be the address in Ontario
2. Book keeping is required, should be retrievable upon request from inspection 
3. Seal of Corporation should be approved by Council 
4. For document to be signed:
	1. CEO 
	2. Any Two officers 
	3. Officer and Director 
5. 4 month after termination of each year corporations report annual results 

***

### Article 3 
1. Fin year is determined by the council 
2. Reports should be send no less than 21 days before annual meeting to members who requested it 
3. Council may without authorization from individual members:
	1. Borrow money on credit from corporation 
	2. Issue, reissue sell or pledge debt 
	3. Give guarantee on behalf of the Corporation 
4. May invest in Funds, according to investing policy 

***
### Article 4
1. General - any registered person should become a member of the corporation 
2. Individual member - broker, natural person. Required to go on all member meetings 
3. Firm member - each broker who is also a sole proprietorship 

##### Appointed individuals - those who aren't member but appointed by LGC

Termination:
1. When body of corporation dissolves 
2. Member of corporation *dissolves*
	1. Cease from qualification standpoint 
	2. fails to pay the bills
3. Termination of rights 
4. Membership is not transferrable 

***
### Article 5
1. Meeting should be held no later than 15 month after previous meeting and no later than 6 month after Corp fiscal year 
2. Meeting should be held in Ontario or in place stated in the notice 
3. Any meeting of members may be adjournment from time to time 

*** 
### Article 6

1. General - council should be the governing body of the corporation 
2. Qualification - not bankrupt, age of majority and is capable 
	1. Individual member - not found of misconduct, incompetence in three years before appointment. Not subject to complaint or claim made by Discipline Committee
3. Council consists from 9 members, 4 appointees, 1 LGC 
4. Office of directors is vacant as soon as someone resign or terminated 
5. Payments of directors is not subject to any member's approval 

***
### Article 7

1. Statutory committee: Q&R, Discipline, Claim
2. Council may establish any other committee and grant it as much power as it choses to 

***
### Article 9

1. Officers: 
	1. President - director, may have power and duties that are specified by the council 
	2. Vice-president - same power and duties if president is absent 
	3. CEO - subject to authority of the council and president. General supervision of business and affairs 
	4. Secretary - gives notices to members and is a custodian for corp. books 
	5. Treasurer - responsible for accounting standards 

2. Council can remove with or without reason any officer 

***
### Article 10 
- Any conflict of interest should be disclosed 
- Conflict should be material 
- Member is excluded from voting in case of conflict 

- Material provided to member shall be destroyed upon termination 
- Any information provided to member about registered person should not be used in any civil action
