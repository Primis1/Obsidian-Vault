### Broker contract - contract between insurer and brokerage
- Describes compensations and scope of services provided by brokerage 

#### Steps in selling a policy 
1. Needs assessment - broker asks sufficient questions to under the nature of the object of insurance and/or risk +objectives for buying insurance 
	1. Consent is completed by signing the document
	2. Calculate pricing 
2. Present coverage options - provide quotes from different insurers. Elaborate on each policy   
3. Complete application form - if client shall proceed with purchase, application with questions should be filled; broker provides assistance but never files the form instead of client  
4. Apply for insurance(fraud detection) - make an assessment of client for fraud 
5. Binding coverage - provides an agreement for temporary coverage. Often limited and can't stay in force for more than 30 days
6. Premium payment 
7. Broker as fiduciary 

#### Renewing policy - typically after 12 month, can be renewed or let it lapse(expire)


#### Claims:
1. Report a claim directly to the insurer 
2. To the brokerage office, then to insurer 

#### Process:
1. Obtain loss details:
	1. Open claim file 
	2. Obtain loss details from claimant 
	3. Complete loss report
2. Verify coverage:
	1. Confirm that policy is in force 
	2. Avoid making any commitment before you check the conditions 
	3. Advice client about exclusions 
3. Provide advice:
	1. Impact of deductible 
	2. How policy is viewed from insurer's POV 
4. Submit claim report 
5. Monitor 
	1. Follow up in 24 h, claim adjusted received it 
	2. Make regular checks 
	3. Provide client with explanations 
6. Close the file:
	1. After loss is settled, insurer sends final report 
	2. File report to close the file
